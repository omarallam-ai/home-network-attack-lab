# Disclaimer

## Legal & Ethical Context

All attacks documented in this repository were performed exclusively on:

- A **personally owned** home network
- **Personally owned** devices connected to that network
- In a **private, isolated environment** with no impact on third parties

No unauthorized systems were accessed. No third-party networks were targeted.
All activity complies with applicable law, including the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)
and equivalent legislation, which prohibits unauthorized access — authorization here
is absolute as the network and all devices belong to me.

---

## Purpose

This repository exists for **educational and portfolio purposes only.** The goal is to
document hands-on learning of offensive techniques in order to build stronger defensive
skills — specifically in detection engineering and SOC workflows.

Each attack documented here is paired with detection indicators and hardening guidance.
The intent is always defensive: understand how attacks work so they can be identified
and prevented.

---

## What Is Not Included

To protect home network privacy, the following are deliberately excluded:

- Screenshots of live attack sessions
- Packet capture files (`.pcap`)
- Password hashes or cracked credentials
- Any personally identifiable network information (SSIDs, MAC addresses, IP ranges)

## Responsible Use

The techniques described in this repository must only be used:

- On networks and devices you own or have explicit written permission to test
- Within the boundaries of applicable law in your jurisdiction
- For educational, research, or authorized penetration testing purposes

Performing these attacks against networks or devices without authorization is illegal
and unethical. Nothing in this repository constitutes authorization or encouragement
to attack systems you do not own.
